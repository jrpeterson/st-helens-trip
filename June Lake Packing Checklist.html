Good to Know — This Trip

  • No cotton anywhere (the bandana is the one exception). Wet cotton stays cold.
  • Few water sources in the blast zone — fill your full water capacity before you head out.
  • No shade once you're in the blast zone — hat, sunscreen, sunglasses, long sleeves.
  • Cool at altitude in the evening and morning — bring your insulation layer and sleeping clothes.
  • Base camp both nights: on Friday you hike with a light daypack — tent and sleep gear stay in camp.
  • Bear-bag nightly: all food and smellables (flavored-drink bottle, toiletries) go up the tree.

Food Rules

  • Aim for 2,500–3,000 calories/day (more for bigger guys); about 500–600 per meal.
  • Only dinner is cooked. Every breakfast, lunch, and snack is no-cook.
  • Portion meals and snacks into labeled freezer bags — e.g. "Name – Fri lunch."
  • All food goes in one sturdy food bag, bear-bagged each night, with room for smellables.
  • Could hit 90° — pick foods that won't melt or crush (dark-chocolate granola over a milk-chocolate bar).
  • Go dense: aim 125–150 cal/oz, favor space-savers (tortillas over bagels), and quick no-assembly foods.
