02 / Friday Route

The Friday Loop

We hike the same loop from both ends and trade cars in the middle. The group driving first leaves their cars at the Ape Canyon Trailhead; the group hiking first collects those cars and drives them back. A second set of keys makes the hand-off work.

Drive first Priests + some Teachers

Hike out, drive around, then hike the loop back to camp.

  1. Hike from camp out to the June Lake Trailhead 1.3 mi
  2. Drive to the Ape Canyon Trailhead, park, leave the cars
  3. Up the Ape Canyon Trail to the Loowit junction 4.2 mi
  4. Turn left / west on the Loowit Trail to June Lake camp 4.7 mi
On foot~10.2 mi

Hike first Deacons + some Teachers

Hike the loop out to the cars, then drive back and walk into camp.

  1. North from June Lake onto the Loowit Trail, heading east toward Ape Canyon 4.7 mi
  2. Down the Ape Canyon Trail to the Ape Canyon Trailhead 4.2 mi
  3. Take the cars the Priests left (use the 2nd set of keys); drive to the June Lake Trailhead
  4. Hike from the trailhead back into camp 1.3 mi
On foot~10.2 mi

Why opposite directions? The two groups pass each other on the Loowit Trail, so the cars never need a separate driver to shuttle them — each group moves the other's vehicles into position. Bring the second set of keys.

03 / Logistics

Parking & Logistics

Two trailheads have parking limits worth planning around.

🚗 June Lake Trailhead

No overnight parking here. The Priests will coordinate ferrying cars the 1.1 miles to the Marble Mountain Sno-Park and back, so we avoid an extra road walk. Plan to drop most people at the trailhead first, then shuttle the cars.

🏔 Marble Mountain Sno-Park

Our overnight lot for most of the cars — 1.1 miles from the June Lake Trailhead.

🚗 Ape Canyon Trailhead

Very limited parking. Use the Lava Canyon lot a few hundred meters away instead — it even has toilets.

A

Bring a dinner to cook

Dinner is the one meal we cook each night — pack a freeze-dried meal to heat up. Everything else (breakfast, lunch, snacks) is no-cook.

B

Breakfast-in-a-bag

A quart freezer bag of your favorite granola plus 3 tablespoons of dehydrated milk. Add water, stir, eat.

C

Bag & label everything

Split meals and snacks into labeled freezer bags (e.g. "Shane – Day 2 lunch"). Keeps calories on track and adds variety.

D

One food bag, hung nightly

All food goes in a single sturdy bag, hung from a tree each night, with extra room for anything smellable.

E

Beat the heat & the squish

Could hit 90°. Skip foods that melt or crush — dark-chocolate granola bars over milk-chocolate bars.

F

Pack small & dense

Ditch bulky packaging and repackage. Favor space-savers — tortillas over bagels — and quick, no-assembly foods.
